MathScience Innovation Center @ VCU, July 2011: Computing with Nanomagnets


MathScience Innovation Center @ VCU, Dec 2012: Nanomagnetic Gates
Dr. Atulasimha and his Ph.D students taught kids about how you can create logic gates with nanomagnets and how they can be used to greatly reduce energy consumption in computers and other electronics that use batteries. Dr. Atul and his students allowed students to see first-hand how magnets can affect each other with attractive and repulsive forces.


In this video, Dr. Atulasimha and his Ph.D students show the children how to use a program where you can cut magnets into multiple segments, and how each of the segments has poles that will attract poles of cut segments next to it.

In this video, Dr. Atulasimha shows his Ph.D students how to use the small magnets to illustrate attraction and repulsion of magnets and how the magnets can create nano gates based on their polarity.

In this video, one of Dr. Atulasimha's Ph. D students, Vimal Sampath, shows one student how to record the inputs and outputs represented by two magnets and record which way the middle magnet faces, which illustrates the attracting and repelling forces of magnets.
