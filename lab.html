a.) Facilities in the Magnetism, Magnetic Materials, & Magnetic Resources Lab

This 600 square feet lab is equipped with instruments for characterization of magnetic materials and multiferroic (elastically coupled magnetostrictive-piezoelectric) materials. Major equipment includes an EM4 Field Control Platform (Lake Shore Cryotronics, Inc.) that contains a large electromagnet and a Lake Shore Cryotronics Inc. large current 642 amplifier capable of driving 60 A current and a Helmholtz coil driven by an AE Techron LVC 2016 Linear Amplifier.

This lab is also equipped with a Lakeshore 475 DSP Gaussmeter, Walker Scientific MF-5D Integrating Fluxmeter for magnetic measurements and Stanford Research Systems SR570 Low-noise current preamplifier and SR560 Low-noise voltage preamplifier for sensitive electrical measurements. Additionally, laser displacement sensors, capacitive sensors, signal conditioners, Vishay P-3 strain conditioner is available for sensitive displacement measurements. A Stanford Research Systems SR785 dual channel dynamics signal analyzer for analyzing frequency response. Several computers and National Instruments Data Acquisition Cards are available for automating custom built magnetic and electric characterization set-ups.

Additionally, the lab is equipped with Trek Model 603, PZD700 and PD08089 amplifiers with maximum voltage outputs of ± 375 V, ± 1.4 kV and ± 10 kV respectively are available for applying large electric fields in the piezoelectric layer to characterize multiferroic materials.

Computing facilities include one dual-core and one quad-core Dell Precision desktops.

  3. Veeco IKONtm AFM system (used for both AFM and MFM studies).
  4. ESCAlab 250 X-ray photoelectron spectrometer.
  5. Anton Parr SAXSESS small angle x-ray scattering system.
  6. PANalytical XRD system
  7. Zeiss Libra 120 TEM
  8. Quantum Design Versalab VSM

The NCC has been designed to ensure that there are no significant vibration and noise sources as well as stray magnetic or electric fields. This will ensure that any instrument located there will be able to achieve its highest resolution capabilities.

d.) Relevant Facilities at NIST (user agreement: VCU and Nanofabrication facility at NIST, Gaithersburg)

PI has access to wide range of e-beam lithography, deposition systems and furnaces. Most of these are also available at VCU but the facilities at NIST, Gaithersburg provide a good back-up. Further, there are some unique magnetic characterization facilities such as Scanning Electron Microscope with Polarization Analysis (SEMPA) which the PI has access to as part of collaboration with NIST personnel.
