Dr. Jayasimha Atulasimha

Dr. Jayasimha Atulasimha is a Qimonda Associate Professor in the Mechanical and Nuclear Engineering Department of the Virginia Commonwealth University. He started there as an assistant professor in August 2008. He received his MS (2003) and PhD (2006) in Aerospace Engineering at the University of Maryland, College Park and B.Tech (2001) in Mechanical Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology, Madras. He worked as a post-doctoral research fellow in Mathematics (2007-2008) at North Carolina State University and Smart Materials (2006-2007) at Royal Military College, Canada. 

His research group studies magnetism, magnetic materials and magnetic devices at multiple length scales. Recent research has focused on complementary experimental and modeling work in the areas of nanomagnetism and spintronics. Applications include nanomagnetic logic and memory devices clocked with strain using multiferroic nanomagnets or nanomagnets clocked with acoustic waves, ultrasensitive strain sensors based on nanowire spin-valves with a magnetostrictive soft later. Emerging areas of interest include novel non-Boolean nanomagnetic architectures and the study of coherent spin transpot and manipulation in nanowire heterostructures. Additionally, his group has occasionally looked at developing microsurgical devices for niche applications with the medical school.
